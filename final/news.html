MAPA DE APAGONES

Care of electrical appliances during blackouts

In view of the frequent blackouts that affect several regions of Ecuador, it is essential to take measures to protect electronic devices and avoid irreversible damage. Here are some practical tips for responsible handling: Disconnect devices: Before a scheduled blackout or when the power supply is interrupted, disconnect televisions, computers, refrigerators and other sensitive appliances to prevent damage from voltage spikes when electricity is restored. Use surge protectors: Installing voltage regulators or surge suppressors can help protect equipment from electrical fluctuations. These tools are especially useful in areas where power outages are recurrent. Avoid overloading connections: During a power outage, avoid plugging too many devices into a single outlet when using generators or batteries. This can generate dangerous overloads. Ensure refrigeration: Keep the refrigerator door closed during a power outage to keep food fresher for longer, reducing the need to constantly open it. Check electrical conditions: After a power outage, make sure that the power supply is stable before reconnecting appliances. This will avoid exposing them to initial fluctuations. With these measures, you will not only protect your appliances and electronics, but also contribute to the responsible use of electricity.
